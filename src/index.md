# Welcome to the Wave Energy Converter Simulator
This site uses a simplified, browser-based simulator to help students and curious learners understand how wave energy converters (WECs) work. 

<div class="callout">
<p>
<strong>What you will explore:</strong> how ocean waves transfer energy to a floating device, why matching the device to the wave frequency matters, and how useful power is extracted. All concepts are introduced with plain-language explanations before any equations appear.
</p>
</div>

## About the Simulator 
The applet models a <em>heaving point absorber</em> — a buoy that bobs up and down as waves pass beneath it. A power take-off system connected to the buoy converts that motion into electricity. The simulation is intentionally simplified: it captures the key physics of resonance and energy extraction while leaving out advanced effects such as radiation damping, diffraction, nonlinear hydrodynamics, and mooring dynamics.
