Welcome to the Wave Energy Converter Simulator

This site uses a simplified, browser-based simulator to help students and curious learners understand how wave energy converters (WECs) work — without needing an engineering degree.

What you will explore: how ocean waves transfer energy to a floating device, why matching the device to the wave frequency matters, and how useful power is extracted. All concepts are introduced with plain-language explanations before any equations appear.

About the Simulator

The applet models a heaving point absorber — a buoy that bobs up and down as waves pass beneath it. A power take-off system connected to the buoy converts that motion into electricity. The simulation is intentionally simplified: it captures the key physics of resonance and energy extraction while leaving out advanced effects such as radiation damping, diffraction, nonlinear hydrodynamics, and mooring dynamics.

Think of it as an interactive thought experiment, not a design tool. It is meant to build intuition — a solid foundation for anyone who wants to learn more.
